How this works

You're auditing a business log. For the highlighted line, decide its context (whose reality is this content in) and its type (what kind of line is it). This is the exact rule set the machine judges use — not a simplification.

context

  • actual — the desk's own record: observations it made, policies and notices in force.
  • story "TITLE" — content from a named work of fiction circulating at the desk (storytelling, not observation).
  • view PARTY — content attributed to a named party: their claim, statement, filing, or projection — logged, but not independently established by the desk.
  • exercise "NAME" — content valid only inside a named planning exercise (assumptions, exercise-scoped policies/notices).

type

  • statement — ordinary assertive content: observations, policies, notices, attributed statements, projections.
  • quote — the line reports a party's own words as direct quoted speech ("…").
  • sarcasm — records an ironic remark; the speaker doesn't mean the literal content. Always actual | sarcasm, even when it mocks someone else's claim.
  • declaration — the line INTRODUCES something rather than asserting content: a story starts circulating, an exercise opens, a party's claims begin being tracked.
  • confirmation — records that an earlier projection was borne out by observation and now enters the desk's own record. Always actual | confirmation.

⚠ The two rules people miss:

  1. A declaration's context is the thing being declared — never actual, even when it reads exactly like an internal policy notice. Example: "As of day 60, the desk opens a separate log for claims attributed to partner_09; going forward, statements from that partner will be filed as its own claims." → view partner_09 | declaration. It LOOKS like the desk describing its own procedure, but it's opening partner_09's view, so the context is partner_09's view, not actual.
  2. A confirmation is always actual, even though it's about someone else's earlier projection. Example: "On day 200, product_04's earlier projection fct_0555 is borne out by observation and now stands entered in the desk's record." → actual | confirmation. The desk is now vouching for it itself — that's the whole point of a confirmation line.

Also: routine feed sources (registry_A, field_report, customer_disclosure, audit_note, partner_feed) are NOT parties with a view — lines sourced from them are actual.

Unsure? Hit the amber flag / unsure button — an honest "don't know" beats a forced guess and doesn't corrupt the data.
