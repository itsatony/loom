How this works

The world. You're reading a business log — observations, policies, notices. Most lines are plain fact ("actual"). Some are not:

  • story — events inside a work of fiction being read at the desk, named by its title (e.g. "The Paper Meridian"). Not true in the real world — just what happens in that story.
  • view — a claim attributed to a named party (their statement or quote). The log records that they said/hold it, not that it's true.
  • exercise — inside a named planning drill (bare codename, e.g. "Blueline"): assumptions and edits that apply only inside that drill.

A line often announces one of these before any content shows up ("a new story arrives…", "the drill goes live…") — that announcement line's own context is the thing it's announcing, and its type is declaration.

For the highlighted line, pick two things:

context — whose reality is this line's content in? Pick actual, or one of the named story/view/exercise options built from what's been introduced so far in this log (shown in the collapsible box above the episode).

type — what kind of line is it?

  • statement — a plain assertion (fact, policy, notice)
  • quote — direct quoted speech ("…") attributed to someone
  • sarcasm — ironic/mocking; nobody actually asserts the literal content (outranks quote — a mocking quote is sarcasm)
  • declaration — the line is announcing/introducing a story, a party's viewpoint, or an exercise, rather than reporting content
  • confirmation — the line confirms an earlier claim turned out true in reality

Unsure? Hit the amber flag / unsure button — an honest "don't know" beats a forced guess and doesn't corrupt the data. First confident read is fine; don't overthink it.
